The emergence of T790M and C797S resistance mutations has led to sequential failure of existing clinical epidermal growth factor receptor tyrosine kinase (EGFR) inhibitors. Currently, few fourth-generation EGFR inhibitors have been developed and no fourth-generation EGFR inhibitor targeting L858R/T790M/C797S mutations passing clinical test. This study establishes a structure-activity relationship model for multi-generation EGFR inhibitors based on a multi-task deep neural network (MT-DNN). MT-DNN achieves an average AUC of 0.79 in cluster cross-validation of internal 2302 EGFR inhibitors. MT-DNN’s performance far exceeds that of other single task models in external testing of 304 fourth-generation EGFR inhibitors after adding multi-generation EGFR inhibitors related tasks. The highly relevant information learned from multi-generation EGFR inhibitors makes MT-DNN significantly different from single target deep neural network (ST-DNN). Superior to previous interpretability models that only provide case studies, the combined application of MT-DNN and SHAP interpretability offers a rigorous structural analysis from a global perspective for development of multi-generation EGFR inhibitors. Overall, MT-DNN can mine core scaffold and important fragments of multi-generation EGFR inhibitors by comparing similarities and differences of multi-tasks, providing valuable information from a structure-activity relationship perspective to address resistant mutation problem.
